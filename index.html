Increase Communications

  • The One Ford Plan and increased communication defined and helped employees understand their jobs better
  • Transparency was important to Mulally and he accomplished it via open and clear communication
  • Through the One Ford Plan and effective communication, Mulally brought to the company's strategic vision, financial health, workforce competitiveness, and product development.
  • He emphasized an employee global team which encouraged accountability and collaboration
  • Mulally exercised great example of downward communication through job instruction; encouraged performance feedback not only from employees, but also performance and product feedback from consumers, ie. Drive One
  • Effective communication raised consumer and employee awareness

Increase Communications

  • Mulally developed a culture of communications across chains of command, fostered cross-communications.
  • “One Team, One Plan, One Goal”
  • Response from employees: better job performance with clear job instruction and performance feedback ; improved employee attitudes - trust in leadership
  • Fresh and timely transfer of information and news to employees from leadership and management
  • Alan Mulally communications team; Ray Day, Group VP, Communications, appointed 2007
  • Continued transparency in communications even during a change in CEO leadership from Mulally to Fields important for employee and consumer awareness.

Increase Communications

  • Communication with consumers improved: Customer awareness via two-way communication; social media participation; Drive One nontraditional marketing; and traditional advertising which includes: corporate-level communications about Ford Motor Company, advertising about our brands and specific products, and dealer-level product advertising. All aid in brand and product awareness.
